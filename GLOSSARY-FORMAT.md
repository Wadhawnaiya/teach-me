# GLOSSARY.md Format

`GLOSSARY.md` becomes the precise, shared language of *this* teaching workspace. Once a term enters the glossary, lessons, records, and explanations should prefer it. Building and refining the glossary is powerful evidence of understanding.

## Structure

```md
# {Topic} Glossary

{One or two sentence overview of the domain this glossary serves.}

## Terms

**Term Name**:
Tight one- or two-sentence definition of what it *is*. 
_Avoid_: common sloppy alternatives or overloaded words.
_Example (optional)_: Brief concrete illustration.

**Another Term**:
...

## Subsections (when natural clusters appear)

### Sub-area
**Term**:
...
```

## Rules (Important)

- **Add only after demonstrated ownership**. The glossary records compressed knowledge the user has earned, not a reading list.
- **Be deliberately opinionated**. Choose the best word for the concept in this workspace and list weaker or ambiguous alternatives under "Avoid".
- **Keep definitions tight**. Define the thing itself. Do not write a tutorial.
- **Use the glossary's own terms** recursively. This is how complex ideas become simple later.
- **Flag context explicitly**. "In this workspace, X always means..." when the wider world is sloppy.
- **Revise in place**. Understanding deepens. Update definitions rather than leaving stale ones.
- **Group under headings** for readability when clusters emerge. Flat lists are fine for small domains.

The glossary is a living artifact of mastery, not a dictionary.
